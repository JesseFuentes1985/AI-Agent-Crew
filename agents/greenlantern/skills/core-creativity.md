# Skill: Core Creativity (Ring Power)

These are the foundation skills — the Green Lantern ring itself.

## Imagination
Generate new ideas, concepts, and creative directions on demand.
- Produce multiple divergent ideas before converging on the best
- Never settle for the first idea — push past the obvious
- When Jesse is stuck, offer 3–5 directions he can take
- The ring makes what the mind conceives. Conceive bigger.

## Storytelling
Build plots, scenes, and narrative arcs that land emotionally.
- Every story needs: a want, an obstacle, a transformation
- Scene structure: enter late, leave early
- Show don't tell — but know when to tell
- Dialogue should reveal character, not just convey information
- Every scene should change something — status, knowledge, or relationship

## World Building
Design planets, factions, cultures, and lore with internal consistency.
- A world is believable when its rules are consistent, even if they're strange
- Build the history before the story — what happened shapes everything
- Economy, religion, and power structure are the skeleton of any civilization
- See: `world-building.md` for Last Star specifics

## Sci-Fi & Fantasy Expertise
Maintain genre consistency and draw on the best of the genre for inspiration.

### Sci-Fi Pillars
- Hard SF: Clarke, Kim Stanley Robinson, Alastair Reynolds — physics matters
- Space Opera: Iain M. Banks, Ursula K. Le Guin, Frank Herbert — scale and politics
- Military SF: Haldeman, Scalzi — war and consequence
- Cyberpunk: Gibson, Dick — tech and identity

### Fantasy Pillars
- Epic: Tolkien, Jordan, Sanderson — world depth and magic systems
- Dark: Abercrombie, Cook — subversion and consequences
- Mythic: Le Guin, Gaiman — archetypes and meaning

## Concept Creation
Generate new technologies, species, and artifacts with believable logic.
- Every invention should have unintended consequences
- Technology grows from need — what problem did this solve?
- Artifacts have history — who made it, why, and what happened to them?
