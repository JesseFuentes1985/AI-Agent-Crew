# Skill: Project Support (Creative Workflow)

Making Jesse's creative process organized, sustainable, and moving forward.

## Idea Organization
Turn scattered ideas into structured notes.

### Capture First, Organize Second
Never filter ideas while capturing — write everything down, judge nothing in the moment.
Use: `workspace-greenlantern/ideas-inbox.md` as the raw dump.

### Organization Framework
| Bucket | What Goes Here |
|---|---|
| 🔥 Active | Working on this now |
| 💡 Develop | Strong idea, needs more work |
| 📦 Archive | Not now, but don't delete |
| ❌ Killed | Tried, didn't work, here's why |

### Weekly Triage (5 min)
1. Move anything from inbox into the right bucket
2. Promote one item from "Develop" to "Active"
3. Archive anything that's been in "Active" for 3+ weeks without movement

## Story Arc Planning
Map multi-book or multi-episode plots.

### The Three-Act Macro Structure
```
Act 1 (25%): Setup — World, character, and problem established
              Inciting incident — everything changes
Act 2 (50%): Confrontation — escalating obstacles
              Midpoint — false victory or defeat, raises stakes
              Dark night of the soul — all seems lost
Act 3 (25%): Resolution — final confrontation
              Climax — decisive moment
              Denouement — new equilibrium
```

### For Series (Multi-Book)
- Each book needs its own arc (setup → confrontation → resolution)
- But each book also advances the overarching series arc
- Book 1: establish the world and the central question
- Book 2: complicate everything, tear apart what was built
- Book 3: answer the central question — at a cost

### Last Star Arc Planning
- What is the central question of the whole series?
- What does the end look like? (Work backwards)
- Which characters don't survive? (Decide early so choices build toward it)

## Creative Task Planning
Suggest next creative steps when Jesse is stuck or doesn't know where to start.

### When Jesse Says "I Don't Know What To Do Next"
Ask:
1. What's the last thing you finished? (establish ground)
2. What's the thing you're most excited about right now? (follow energy)
3. What's been sitting undone the longest? (clear the backlog)

## Lore Consistency Checking
Prevent contradictions before they become canon problems.

### The Consistency Check Process
1. State the new element being added
2. Cross-reference against: existing factions, timeline, tech rules, character histories
3. Flag any conflicts
4. Propose resolution (change the new element OR retcon the old one — pick the better story)

### Common Contradiction Types
- **Timeline:** Event A happened before Event B, but B causes A
- **Power scale:** Character X couldn't do Y in chapter 2, but does Y in chapter 8
- **Geography:** Location is north of X in one scene, south in another
- **Character voice:** Character says something completely out of character without explanation
- **Tech rules:** Technology does something it was established it cannot do

### The Canon Doc
Everything confirmed as canon goes in Notion.
Anything that contradicts Notion needs a conversation before it becomes real.
